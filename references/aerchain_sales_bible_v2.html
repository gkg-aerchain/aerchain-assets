Intelligent routing engine that directs every procurement request to the right channel based on policies, spend value, category, and complexity. Invisible to users, always working.

DAY 1: Discovery & Current State
What Happens on Day 1

Activity 1: Structured Discovery Questionnaire

Format: 50 points total, 25 PRE-FILLED from Stage 0/1 research

Position: "We come prepared. Please confirm these. The remaining 25, we discuss."

  • Organization structure and stakeholder mapping
  • Current procurement volume and transaction types
  • Spend by category and geographic distribution
  • System landscape and integration points
  • Existing processes and approval workflows
  • Pain points and system gaps identified
  • Timeline and resource availability
  • Success metrics and business objectives
  • Security, compliance, and data requirements
  • Executive sponsorship and budget authority

Activity 2: Current Process Walk

Flow: PR intake → sourcing → evaluation → negotiation → award → PO → payment

Output: Map every step, handoff, bottleneck. Count: steps, hours, people, systems.

  • PR creation (who, how, approval chain)
  • Routing to procurement (SLA, waiting time)
  • Sourcing event creation (template, timing)
  • Vendor engagement (outreach, response time)
  • Response collection (format, tracking)
  • Technical analysis (tools, hours)
  • Evaluation (scorecards, approvers, timeline)
  • Negotiation (iterations, decision makers)
  • Award (justification, approval)
  • PO creation and issuance (systems, cycle time)
  • Post-award (contract, payment, reconciliation)

Activity 3: Stakeholder Interviews (30 min each)

Key Questions by Role:

  • Procurement Lead: "What's your biggest daily pain? Manual work you'd love to eliminate?"
  • Finance/CFO: "Where do you lose money? Maverick spend? Payment delays? Contract non-compliance?"
  • IT/CIO: "What integrations are non-negotiable? Data security requirements? System constraints?"
  • Business Users: "How do you request spend today? What's broken in that process?"
  • Vendor Manager (if applicable): "Vendor adoption challenges? Portal usage today?"

Activity 4: Pain Point Quantification

Goal: Translate pain into dollar value

  • Cycle time: X days of processing cost → $Y in working capital, delayed decision-making
  • Maverick spend: X% of spend unmanaged → $Y in missed negotiations, compliance risk
  • Vendor adoption: X% of vendors use portal, Y% manual follow-up → $Z per month in labor
  • Audit rework: X hours per quarter in compliance audits → $Y in internal cost + risk
  • Manual data entry: X transactions × Y minutes each → $Z per year in labor
  • System sprawl: Spreadsheets, email trails, tribal knowledge → cost of errors + knowledge loss
End of Day 1 Deliverable: Detailed current state documentation. Quantified pain list. Stakeholder alignment on biggest opportunities.

STAGE 6

Post-Demo Closing Motion — Multi-Threaded Sales Execution

Duration: 4-8 weeks

Overview

The Post-Demo Closing Motion is where enterprise deals are won or lost. The prospect has seen the product, believes in the value, and wants to proceed — but they haven't committed. Multiple stakeholders need to be aligned, internal champions need to be armed, IT needs to clear security, references need to validate claims, and executives need to see the strategic picture. This stage is multi-threaded: 6 workstreams run in parallel, each with its own timeline and owner.

THIS IS ENTIRELY NEW. This is the most critical and often longest stage in enterprise sales — the period between "they want to buy" and "they've committed to buy."

Visual Overview: 6 Parallel Threads

6A: Champion Enablement (Week 1-2) → 6B: Multi-Stakeholder Threading (Week 1-6) → 6C: IT/Security Evaluation (Week 1-8) → 6D: Reference Calls (Week 2-4) → 6E: Executive Alignment (Week 3-6) → 6F: POC/Pilot Decision (Week 4-8, conditional)

These threads run in PARALLEL, not sequentially. The sales team must manage all threads simultaneously.

6A: Champion Enablement

Duration: Week 1-2 | Owner: Sales Lead

The internal champion (usually CPO or VP Sourcing) must sell Aerchain inside their own organization. They'll face questions from Finance, IT, CEO, and procurement team leads. They need:

Champion Enablement Checklist

Your champion is your internal sales team. If they can't sell Aerchain when you're NOT in the room, the deal dies. Invest in arming them.

Duration: 4-8 weeks (often the longest thread — can block deals) | Owner: Solutions Architect

Start IT/Security evaluation as EARLY as possible — ideally during Stage 2/3, running in parallel with the workshop. Don't wait until Stage 6. The earlier you start, the less it blocks the deal.
