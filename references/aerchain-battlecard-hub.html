FUD Seeding Toolkit
Plant strategic uncertainty early in Ariba-heavy accounts. Ask these in first 20 minutes of discovery.
⏱ Time-to-Value Risk
“What’s your current go-live estimate for Ariba, and who owns the implementation?”
Average Ariba implementation: 9–18 months. Uncover SI dependency and timeline risk early. Make them say the number out loud.
❄ AI Strategy Freeze
“What’s your team’s plan for the Next-Gen Ariba migration — has SAP given you a firm destination?”
Next-Gen Ariba announced Oct 2025. Classic customers face forced migration. No stable roadmap yet. Every AI upgrade requires BTP expansion.
💰 Hidden TCO
“When you modeled total cost, did the number include SAP BTP licensing, SI fees, and the annual support retainer?”
Real TCO is often 2–3x the quoted SaaS fee when BTP, SI fees, and annual support are included. Surface the full picture before they sign anything.
👻 Adoption Graveyard
“What’s your actual procurement portal adoption rate across BUs — not just procurement power users?”
Industry median Ariba adoption: 40–60% of intended user base. Complex UX kills self-service. Make the champion acknowledge the number.
🔒 Vendor Lock-In
“If SAP changes licensing in 18 months, what’s your exit strategy — or are you effectively locked in?”
SAP ecosystem lock-in is real. Every upgrade cycle is a project. Force the conversation about dependency before it becomes your competitor’s defense.
🤖 The 2027 Problem
“When Joule reaches full autonomy — on roadmap for 2027 — what’s the implementation overhead to unlock it?”
Aerchain is autonomous today. Ariba’s autonomous future requires S/4HANA + BTP + Joule alignment. Plant the comparison: GPS vs. self-driving car.

Objection Handling

Q
“We’ve already invested heavily in SAP Ariba — we can’t switch.”
►

We’re not asking you to switch — we’re asking you to augment. Aerchain sits above your ERP and Ariba as an autonomous AI orchestration layer. NSE kept their SAP backbone and added Aerchain on top. The question isn’t either/or. It’s: what is your Ariba actually covering versus what’s still done on Excel and email? Aerchain solves the part Ariba was never designed for: unstructured, complex, high-touch sourcing at scale.

Q
“SAP just launched Joule — isn’t that basically the same thing?”
►

Joule is an AI assistant — it suggests the next step, and a human executes. Aerchain is an AI agent — it executes the entire workflow autonomously from intake to PO. Joule requires BTP, Work Zone, CDM APIs, and S/4HANA to function at all. Aerchain deploys in 4–6 weeks. The difference is between a GPS that gives you directions and a self-driving car that drives.

Q
“Ariba has 4 million suppliers — how can you match that network?”
►

Aerchain connects to the Ariba network — we don’t compete with it. We also connect to your existing ERP, your current supplier lists, and 30+ finance and ERP integrations. Your suppliers don’t change. Your process transforms. And for tail spend — the 70–80% of spend outside your Ariba catalog — Aerchain’s agents find and qualify new suppliers automatically.

Q
“SAP is enterprise-grade. Can a startup handle critical procurement?”
►

Aerchain holds ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type I & II, GDPR, and VAPT certification — the same stack required by NSE, India’s national stock exchange under SEBI oversight. $20B+ in managed spend across 30+ countries. Reliance, Unilever, Danone, ABInBev. These are not experiments. The real risk question is: what’s the downside of committing to a vendor that is in the middle of a forced architecture migration?
