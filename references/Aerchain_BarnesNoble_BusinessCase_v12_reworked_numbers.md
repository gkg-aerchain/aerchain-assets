## Estimated Orchestration to Aerchain Channels (7 channels)

**Key principle line stays the same.** Re-size all 7 cards as follows:

### 1. Strategic Sourcing — 30% of spend
- **Spend:** $170M – $190M  *(was $0.7B–$0.9B)*
- **Transactions:** 1.5K – 2.5K *(was 27K–39K)*
- **Savings:** 5% – 10% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Consolidates B&N's largest indirect contracts — facilities & maintenance frameworks, 3PL/inbound freight, IT platforms (POS, e-commerce, telecom), and major capex (store builds, remodels) — into structured RFX events. With ~1,200 suppliers and a 4-person team, the leverage from competitively bidding the top ~50 contracts is the single biggest savings driver.

### 2. Tactical Sourcing — 21% of spend
- **Spend:** $120M – $135M  *(was $0.5B–$0.6B)*
- **Transactions:** 2.5K – 4K *(was 18K–26K)*
- **Savings:** 4% – 8% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Recurring GNFR (cleaning, store services, marketing campaigns, non-critical IT, training). Lighter-weight 3-quote events with auto-routing reduce cycle time and tighten preferred-supplier compliance — critical when the indirect team is only 4 people.

### 3. Autonomous Sourcing — 10% of spend
- **Spend:** $55M – $65M  *(was $0.24B–$0.28B)*
- **Transactions:** 4K – 6K *(was 9K–13K)*
- **Savings:** 6% – 12% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Standard store supplies, defined logistics lanes, recurring marketing collateral — categories with repeatable patterns where AI agents can run RFQs, evaluate bids, and recommend awards with human-in-the-loop only on exceptions. This is where the "60–70% of transaction volume without buyer involvement" promise lives.

### 4. Catalog — 14% of spend
- **Spend:** $80M – $95M  *(was $0.36B–$0.42B)*
- **Transactions:** 10K – 15K *(was 14K–20K)*
- **Savings:** 3% – 6% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Guided buying for store consumables, office supplies, IT peripherals, and standard local services — driven directly to ~600 store managers under the cluster model. Reduces free-text spend and improves contract utilization without slowing the front line.

### 5. Self-Service PO — 10% of spend
- **Spend:** $55M – $65M  *(was $0.24B–$0.28B)*
- **Transactions:** 9K – 12K *(unchanged range)*
- **Savings:** 2% – 5% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Local store managers raise policy-controlled POs within budget and supplier guardrails — converts today's non-PO/manual store requests into structured transactions. Preserves cluster-model autonomy while giving central procurement visibility.

### 6. Non-PO — 10% of spend
- **Spend:** $55M – $65M  *(was $0.24B–$0.28B)*
- **Transactions:** 5K – 7.5K *(was 9K–13K)*
- **Savings:** 4% – 7% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Structured intake, enriched coding, and policy checks for unavoidable non-PO categories — utilities, store leases (third-party), local fees, community-event spend. Data feeds back into strategic and tactical sourcing.

### 7. RC Invoice — 5% of spend
- **Spend:** $25M – $35M  *(was $0.12B–$0.14B)*
- **Transactions:** 1.5K – 2.5K *(was 4K–7K)*
- **Savings:** 3% – 6% *(unchanged)*
- **Reasoning:** Subscriptions, telco, SaaS, utility recurring commitments matched against rates/volumes/terms automatically. Exceptions flagged; standard invoices flow straight through.
