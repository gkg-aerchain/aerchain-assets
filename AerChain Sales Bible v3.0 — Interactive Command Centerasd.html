Lead Generation & Market Positioning
Ongoing · Owner: Marketing + SDR + Partnerships · 9 Collaterals

5 Input Channels

  • 0A Cold Outbound — Target: CPOs, VP Sourcing. Research company → build L1 business case → send email snapshot (1-page value summary + QR to full L1). 5-8% conversion
  • 0B Inbound (Website/Content) — Website inquiry → qualify → route to sales. Collateral: "Sentient Procurement" whitepaper, blog posts. 15-20% MQL→SQL
  • 0C Events + CEO Dinners — Pre-build custom L1 reports for ALL attendees → print as brochures → hand out at booth. Key insight: custom L1 for each company is the differentiator, not generic brochures. 10-15% → call
  • 0D Partner Referrals — Deloitte, Infosys, Accenture, EY. Partner identifies client need → introduces AerChain → co-sell. 25-30% warm conversion
  • 0E Customer Referrals — Existing champion network. Procurement is a close-knit industry — 1 success story unlocks 10 deals. 30-40% (highest quality)

Pre-Work Checklist

  • Research company: annual report, SEC filings, procurement maturity, industry position
  • Build L1 business case (60% accurate): industry benchmarks, estimated spend, estimated events/year
  • Identify likely pain points based on industry vertical + company size + known systems
  • Stakeholder mapping: CPO, CFO, CIO, procurement team leads
  • Competitive intel: what systems they currently use (Ariba? Coupa? In-house?)

Exit Gate

  • Prospect agrees to discovery call → Move to Stage 1
  • No → Nurture Track 0: monthly thought leadership, quarterly L1 refresh, event invitations. Re-engage triggers: new funding round, leadership change, fiscal year start. Timeline: 6-12 months.
0.5
Pre-Discovery Preparation
3-5 days · Owner: Sales Lead (Harsha/Gaurav) · 7 Collaterals

Purpose

The bridge between lead gen and discovery. This is where you transform a cold lead into an informed, prepared first conversation.

Pre-Call Research Checklist

  • Company annual report — Revenue, margins, growth trajectory
  • SEC filings (10-K, 8-K) — Strategic initiatives, acquisitions, risks
  • Recent M&A activity — Who they've bought, who might be buying them
  • Leadership changes — New CFO? New CIO? New procurement leadership? (LinkedIn, news)
  • Analyst reports — Industry position, competitive landscape

L1 Business Case Preparation (60% Accurate)

  • Estimated annual spend: Research industry benchmarks for company size + sector. Use SEC revenue data as proxy. Formula: Revenue × procurement intensity % (e.g., manufacturing 8-12%, tech 3-5%)
  • Industry benchmarks: Average sourcing cycle time for their industry, typical events/year, vendor consolidation targets
  • Estimated events/year: Based on company size and industry. Reliance: 300+/week. Mid-market: 50-100/year. SMB: 10-20/year
  • Current system assessment: If public data available, note their existing procurement platform

Custom Email Snapshot Creation

  • 1-page value summary (custom per company) covering: "Here's what we see as your biggest opportunity in procurement" + 2-3 tailored data points
  • Add QR code linking to full L1 business case (PDF hosted or embedded)
  • Signature: "Looking forward to discussing on [proposed date]"

Stakeholder Mapping

  • CPO (Chief Procurement Officer): Title search on LinkedIn, note if one doesn't exist (red flag or opportunity?)
  • CFO / Finance: Name, tenure, recent moves
  • CIO / IT Director: Systems owner, integration authority
  • Procurement team leads: Directors, managers — who are the key people to get in the room early?
  • Business owners: COO, plant managers, regional heads — who initiates spend requests?

Competitive Intelligence Gathering

  • What systems do they use today? Ariba? Coupa? Jaggr? Custom SAP? Spend Visibility tool? Job postings often reveal this.
  • Procurement maturity: Manual Excel-based? Partially automated? AI-native?
  • Known pain points: LinkedIn posts, company interviews, news articles about procurement challenges
  • Recent initiatives: Digital transformation? Cost reduction? Supply chain resilience? Integration push?

Exit Gate

  • Research complete + L1 draft ready → Stage 1 (send meeting invite with snapshot + link to L1)
  • Research insufficient or company doesn't fit ICP → Nurture Track or reject
1
Discovery Call + L1 Business Case
45-60 min · Owner: Sales Lead (Harsha/Gaurav) · 9 Collaterals

[0-3 min] Opening — Choose Language Register

  • CPO / VP Sourcing: "We were the end user. We managed procurement for 50+ enterprises. Handled 5 crores/month, 150-200 orders/day. We saw every tool fail. We built AerChain to be what those tools should have been."
  • MD / CFO / CIO: "Procurement is a business support function. Today it operates like a ticketing system — high volume, low value. What if we could move your team from 'processor' to 'policy owner and optimizer'?"
  • Mixed (CPO + CFO): Start executive register, shift to procurement when CPO engages with questions.

[3-5 min] Credentials (Keep Tight)

  • Founded by practitioners — managed procurement for 50+ enterprises
  • Gaurav brought Reliance (India's largest conglomerate)
  • Live with: Reliance (300+ events/week), Unilever (negotiation agent live), Danone (9.5/10 language), ABInBev (10 countries), NSE (regulated, SOC2-cleared), Meta
  • 80+ team, $20B+ spend managed, 140+ AI agents under 5 themes

[5-15 min] Problem Diagnosis Questions

  • "How many sourcing events do you run per year? Per week?"
  • "What's your average sourcing cycle time, PR to PO?"
  • "What systems are you using today? Ariba? Coupa? In-house?"
  • "What's your biggest pain? Cycle time? Vendor adoption? Compliance? Cost?"
  • "Who initiates spend in your organization? How do they do it today?"
  • "How do you handle strategic vs. tactical vs. routine sourcing today?"

[15-30 min] 4 Value Pillars

  • Productivity: "80% of procurement work is grunt. We eliminate it. Your team does 4-5x more strategic work." Proof: Reliance — 300+ events/week at scale
  • Cycle Time: "Sourcing from 60 days to 25 days. PR-to-PO from 45 to 15-20 days." Proof: Reliance — 67% reduction in PR-to-PO
  • Financial Impact: "1%+ savings from market intelligence, consolidation, and operational efficiency." Proof: Industry benchmarks + credit model
  • Future-Proofing: "AI-native. Every transaction learns. System gets smarter. Compliance by design." Proof: NSE — 6-7 month security eval cleared

[40-45 min] Close — Concrete Next Step

  • NEVER end with "Let's see how we proceed." ALWAYS propose specific next step with DATE.
  • Script: "Based on what we've discussed, I'd recommend a deep-dive demo with your sourcing team — [specific date within 2 weeks]. Should we lock [date]?"
  • If they resist: "No pressure. Can I send you a recorded demo walkthrough this week? You review async, and we reconnect when you're ready."

Top Objection Responses

  • "Different from Ariba/Coupa?" — "Not here to replace Ariba. Tell me an area that feels difficult. Try AerChain for that. We integrate."
  • "Can your AI really do this?" — "Human-in-the-loop always. AI assists, you decide. Gets better with every transaction."
  • "What does this cost?" — "Consumption-based, starts at $50K+. For every $1 invested, $8-10 savings Year 1. 8-month payback."
  • "Small company, can you deliver?" — "Reliance trusts us. NSE cleared us after 6-7 months security eval. 80+ team, $20B+ spend managed."

Exit Gate

  • Client agrees to deep-dive demo → Stage 2 (propose date within 2 weeks)
  • No → Nurture Track 1: recorded demo + biz case updates + reference calls, 3-6 months
1.5
Post-Discovery Follow-Up
48 hours max · Owner: Sales Lead (Harsha/Gaurav) · 6 Collaterals

Purpose

Convert discovery momentum into demo commitment. The 48 hours after discovery are make-or-break.

Within 24 Hours: Post-Call Outreach

  • Email #1 — Meeting Recap: "Thank you for the call. Here's what we heard and what we're proposing next."
  • Key points summary:
    • Top 3 pain points they identified (in their words, verbatim)
    • How we address each one (concise, not verbose)
    • "This is what resonated most with us from our conversation"
  • Proposed next steps: "We recommend a deep-dive demo with your sourcing team [DATE]. Should we lock that?" (ALWAYS propose specific date, not "let's connect next week")

L1 Business Case Refinement

  • Update L1 with actual data from discovery: actual annual spend (if shared), actual sourcing cycle time, actual event volume
  • Replace estimated metrics with REAL numbers — this shifts from 60% to 85%+ confidence
  • Prepare 2-3 custom value snapshots showing: "If you improved from 60-day cycle to 30-day cycle, here's the working capital impact"
  • Share refined L1 in next email OR async link before demo

Internal Alignment: Brief Presales Engineer

  • Share discovery notes: What did they say are their biggest pain points?
  • Configure demo scenarios: "They run 50-100 events/year, mostly category-focused sourcing. Show: speed-to-RFQ, bidder management, document automation."
  • Prep talking points: Presales engineer should know: their current system, their volume, their pain before the demo
  • Allocate time: "We have their CFO for 15 min — what's the highest-impact thing to show them?"

Demo Preparation: Configure Aiera Copilot Scenarios

  • Set up sandbox with scenarios matching their use cases: If they mentioned vendor consolidation, build a consolidation RFQ scenario
  • Pre-load sample data: procurement templates, vendor lists, compliance policies (anonymized from existing clients if applicable)
  • Prepare 2-3 alternate paths: "If they ask about integration, we have this path. If they ask about AI governance, we pivot to this."

Stakeholder Expansion: Request CPO/CFO/CIO Introductions

  • In follow-up call: "Who else should see this demo? We've found CFO and CIO involvement early accelerates everything."
  • Get specific introductions: Ask champion to make email intro to next stakeholder BEFORE demo (don't just add to calendar invite)
  • Pre-brief champion: "Here's what we'll show the CFO. This is the number they care about." — Get buy-in first

Exit Gate

  • Demo date locked + L1 refined + all key stakeholders confirmed → Stage 2 (Deep Dive Demo)
  • No response → Send recorded demo link + 3-day wait. If no response, move to Nurture Track 1
2
Deep Dive Demo — Proof of Capability
60-90 min (up to 2 hrs) · Owner: Presales Engineer + Sales Lead · 8 Collaterals

9 Intelligent Pathways Framework

  • 1. Catalog Self-Service (12%) — Pre-negotiated, policy-compliant items via self-service
  • 2. Framework Agreements (10%) — Pre-negotiated rate contracts for recurring services
  • 3. RC Invoice Terms (6%) — Recurring invoices for subscriptions, leases
  • 4. Non-PO Spend (5%) — True exceptions only (regulatory, taxes, donations)
  • 5. Self-Service PO (12%) — Low-risk, low-value within defined budgets
  • 6. Autonomous Negotiation (15%) — AI-powered for mid-value repeatable purchases
  • 7. Autonomous Sourcing (15%) — AI-driven automated sourcing for spot buys
  • 8. Tactical Sourcing (15%) — Mid-value through guided RFQs and templates
  • 9. Strategic Sourcing (10%) — High-value, complex multi-year events

16-Step Demo Flow

  • Step 1: Aiera Copilot WOW — Natural language → structured procurement. No forms, no menus. "Business user types: 'I need 100 units of X for Project Y'"
  • Step 2: Orchestration Engine WOW — Based on YOUR policies, spend routes automatically to the right channel
  • Step 3: RFI Creation — AI generates entire questionnaire. 15 min instead of 2 days
  • Step 4: Vendor Experience — Simple guided interface. Download to Excel → work offline → upload. Vendor does everything in 15 min
  • Step 5: AI Technical Analysis — Reads EVERYTHING vendors submitted. Company profile, strengths, weaknesses, red flags. "2 min vs. 2 days"
  • Step 6: Email Deliverability Tracking — Sent → Delivered → Opened → Clicked (full chain per vendor)
  • Step 7: RFP + AI Normalization TOP WOW — Vendors submit in ANY format (PDF, PPT, Word, Excel). AI reads all, normalizes pricing, side-by-side comparison. "10 vendors, 10 formats. AI normalizes all."
  • Step 8: Evaluations — Assign evaluators to sections. Rate + shortlist. Delegation if unavailable
  • Step 9: RFQ Creation — 3 types: Standard, Grouped/Package (BOQ), Variant. Multi-currency support
  • Step 10: Sealed Bids — 4 trigger types: Deadline, Foreclose, Post-technical-eval, Bid Opening Committee. Chinese Wall enforced
  • Step 11: Vendor Alternatives — Vendor suggests better option with justification, pricing, docs
  • Step 12: AI Negotiation TOP WOW — Target + starting price per line item. AI drafts negotiation email with per-line-item reasoning. "AI tells you what to aim for and WHY"
  • Step 13: Live Auction — Real-time bidding, multiple formats, reserve prices, auto-extensions
  • Step 14: Scenario Builder — Build multiple award scenarios (lowest cost, best quality, split). Compare and auto-allocate
  • Step 15: Award + Auto PO — Mix and match across vendors per line item. On approval → AUTO-CREATES PO IN ERP
  • Step 16: Audit Trail & Collaboration — Full logging. Internal + external chat. Thread-based conversations. Amendment versioning

Industry Adaptation

  • Oil & Gas — Steps 9,10,12. Complex specs, sealed bids, technical drawings
  • FMCG — Steps 2,9,12. High volume, grouped RFQ, multi-country, multi-language
  • Manufacturing — Steps 9,10,11. BOQ structure, vendor alternatives, constraints
  • Financial Services — Steps 5,6,10,16. Compliance, sealed bids, audit trail
  • Tech/Services — Steps 1,2,7,12. Copilot self-service, AI normalization
  • Pharma — Steps 3,8,10,16. Regulatory questionnaires, evaluations

Exit Gate

  • Client agrees to workshop + 2+ use cases → Stage 3
  • Yes + IT clearance needed → Parallel IT eval (4-8 wks) + Stage 3
  • No → Nurture Track 2

Post-Demo Momentum
3-5 days · Owner: Sales Lead + Presales Engineer · 8 Collaterals

Purpose

Bridge from "that was impressive" to "let's get serious." Most deals die in this gap.

Within 24 Hours: Post-Demo Outreach

  • Send demo recording: "Here's the full recording. You can watch at your own pace and share internally."
  • Key highlights document:
    • Timeline showing what we showed (e.g., "Min 12-15: Multi-vendor comparison, live data refresh")
    • 3 specific value points tailored to their pain from the discovery call
    • Sample ROI calculation: "If you accelerate sourcing by 30%, here's the working capital benefit"
  • Tone: "Here's what we heard resonated. Here's what it means for you. Let's talk about next steps."

Capture WOW Moments

  • Immediately after demo: "What moment got the strongest reaction?" (From presales engineer perspective)
  • Document: "They leaned in when we showed vendor intelligence." "CFO's eyes widened at the cycle time reduction calculation."
  • Use these moments in sales narratives: "The thing that resonated most with your team was..."

Workshop Pitch: "3-Day On-Site Value Engineering"

  • Positioning: "We'd like to come on-site for 3 days. We'll come prepared — 50% of the discovery is already done."
  • What we'll deliver:
    • Day 1: Current state walkthrough (with our 50-point checklist pre-filled)
    • Day 2: Hands-on configuration — set up YOUR templates, YOUR vendors, YOUR workflows
    • Day 3: Live scenario testing, stakeholder sign-off on scope, executive presentation
  • Why 3 days vs. 1-day workshop: "We've found that 1 day is a tour. 3 days is transformation. You come out with a configured system and a clear roadmap."

Internal Buy-In Expansion: Get Champion to Invite CFO/CIO

  • Call champion: "Your CFO needs to be in the workshop. Here's why: [explain working capital impact]. Can you invite them?"
  • Pre-brief champion on CFO talking points: "The financial impact comes from: (1) 30-40 day cycle reduction = working capital. (2) 1% tail spend elimination. Here's your business case."
  • CIO involvement: "Your IT team needs to know: we handle integration with [their systems]. No infrastructure lift required."

Technical Pre-Qualification: Begin IT/Security Parallel Track

  • Enterprise deals ($500K+): Initiate IT security evaluation NOW (don't wait for Stage 4)
  • Security checklist: SOC2 Type II — check. Data residency — check. API encryption — check. Share with their CISO
  • Integration requirements: "What systems do you need to integrate with? ERP? AP? Let's confirm APIs and timelines."

Exit Gate

  • Workshop dates locked + stakeholder list confirmed (CPO, CFO, CIO, Procurement lead) → Stage 3 (Workshop)
  • No movement within 1 week → Move to Nurture Track 2: reference calls, case study reviews, check-in calls monthly

Workshop + L2 Business Case — Value Engineering & Scope Lock
3 days on-site preferred · Owner: Full AerChain Team (5 people) · 13 Collaterals

Day 1 — Discovery & Current State

  • 50-point structured discovery questionnaire — 25 PRE-FILLED from Stage 0/1 research. Position: "We come prepared. Please confirm these. The remaining 25, we discuss."
  • Current process walk: Map every step: PR intake → sourcing → evaluation → negotiation → award → PO → payment. Count steps, hours, people, systems involved. Identify every handoff and bottleneck.
  • Stakeholder interviews (30 min each): Procurement team ("What's your biggest daily pain?"), Finance ("Where do you lose money?"), IT ("What integrations are non-negotiable?"), Business users ("How do you request spend today?")
  • Pain point quantification in $: Cycle time costs in expedited shipments, maverick spend in missed savings, vendor adoption in manual follow-up, audit rework in hours per quarter

Day 2 — Value Stream Mapping & Custom Demo

  • Value stream map: Current 50+ steps / 60+ days → AerChain 15-20 steps / 20-30 days. Calculate savings: X hours/transaction × Y transactions/year
  • Custom demo with THEIR data: Orchestration with THEIR thresholds, RFI/RFP/RFQ with THEIR T&Cs, THEIR categories, THEIR vendor types, THEIR technical drawings
  • Integration architecture review: AerChain ERP (SAP/Oracle) CLM Vendor master. Data flow: IN → Process → OUT → ERP → Feedback loop

Day 3 — Business Case & Scope Lock

  • L2 Business Case (80% accurate): Current state pain (quantified from Day 1), AerChain impact per use case, 3-year financial model with sensitivities, ROI: Year 1/2/3, IRR, Payback
  • 3 Measurable Outcomes — LOCKED Both sides sign off: Not aspirational. Achievable. Trackable. Template: "By [date], we achieve: (1) ___, (2) ___, (3) ___"
  • Example outcomes: "By Month 6, 65% of tail spend routed through Orchestrator with 4-day cycle" / "By Month 9, 50% automation of indirect sourcing, 45→20 day cycle" / "By Month 12, 8% procurement efficiency, $12M net savings"
  • 9 Savings Levers: Demand Aggregation 4-7%, Contract Compliance 3-6%, Category Consolidation 3-7%, PPV 3-6%, Supplier Rationalization 3-5%, Channel Optimization 1-3%. Net: 2-3% baseline → 6-8% with AerChain
  • Wave-based rollout: Wave 1 (tail spend), Wave 2 (indirect), Wave 3 (direct/strategic)

Exit Gate

  • L2 accepted + scope agreed + large deal ($500K+) → Stage 4 (Spend Analysis)
  • Standard deal → Stage 5 or Stage 6
  • No → Nurture Track 3: exec escalation, 1-2 months
3.5
Post-Workshop Value Lock
1-2 weeks post-workshop · Owner: Sales Lead + Presales + Solution Architect · 9 Collaterals

Purpose

Lock in the value narrative before commercial discussion begins. This is where the deal becomes "real" in the client's mind.

L2 Business Case Delivery

  • Timeline: Send within 1 week of workshop while energy is high
  • Content: Everything from discovery + workshop learnings: actual spend data, actual processes, actual pain quantified
  • 3 scenarios: Conservative (30% improvement), base case (45% improvement), aggressive (60% improvement) — let client pick which to pursue
  • Custom financial model: Their revenue, their margin, their working capital cycle. Not a template.
  • Sign-off: Both sides agree "this is realistic" before moving to commercial discussion

3 Measurable Outcomes: Signed Agreement

  • What are the 3 things we MUST achieve? E.g.:
    • Outcome 1: "Reduce sourcing cycle from 60 days to 35 days (41% improvement)"
    • Outcome 2: "Achieve 95% on-time delivery rate for all sourcing events"
    • Outcome 3: "Identify and consolidate $10M in tail spend (8% savings)"
  • How we measure: Sourcing cycle = PR date to event close. On-time = event completion within committed timeline. Savings = third-party verified.
  • Both sides sign: "We commit to these outcomes. We'll track them quarterly."

Scope Lock Document: P0/P1 vs. P2/P3

  • Phase 1 (P0 — Critical): Core procurement workflow, vendor intelligence, RFQ automation, basic reporting
  • Phase 1 (P1 — Important): API integrations with ERP, contract repository, basic analytics
  • Phase 2 (P2 — Nice to have): Advanced forecasting, supplier risk, advanced reporting, governance workflows
  • Phase 3 (P3 — Future): Predictive sourcing, autonomous negotiation, advanced AI agents
  • Clear and signed: "This is what's in. This is what's out. This is the scope we'll deliver on. Changes to scope = timeline/budget impact."

Wave-Based Rollout Plan

  • Wave 1 (Tail Spend): Low-risk, high-volume category. Fast win. "This category represents 5% of spend, 40% of events. We prove value here first."
  • Wave 2 (Indirect): Facility services, travel, consulting. Low complexity, broad adoption, quick ROI proof.
  • Wave 3 (Direct/Strategic): High-value sourcing events, vendor negotiations, strategic categories. Full feature set deployed.
  • Timeline: Wave 1 = months 1-2. Wave 2 = months 3-5. Wave 3 = months 6-9.

Internal Presentation Support: Help Champion Present to Board/Leadership

  • Pitch deck prep: We draft the first version. Champion refines to match internal politics/language.
  • Key slides: Problem statement, how we solve it, 3 measurable outcomes, financial impact (L2), implementation timeline, risk mitigation
  • Executive summary (1-pager): "Here's what this means for the company. Here's how we measure success. Here's the investment and ROI."
  • Approval call: We listen to champion present. Immediately after, we gather feedback and refine financial model if needed.

Exit Gate

  • L2 accepted + 3 Measurable Outcomes signed + scope locked → Stage 4 (if $500K+) OR Stage 6 (if $100K-$500K)
  • Champion can't get board approval or L2 rejected → Refine model or nurture until next fiscal year
4
Spend Analysis + L3 Business Case
2-4 weeks · Conditional: deals $500K+ or CFO requires deeper validation · 10 Collaterals

AI Spend Analysis Pipeline

  • Client uploads 1-2 years of spend data → Data quality assessment: completeness, missing fields, duplicates → Report shared
  • Category breakdown: Top 10-20 categories by spend, vendor concentration & dependency analysis, geographic distribution
  • Maverick spend identification: Off-contract, non-compliant purchases quantified in $
  • Market benchmarking: AI compares their rates vs. industry benchmarks

Savings Waterfall & L3 Output

  • Market intelligence: 0.3% · Vendor consolidation: 0.5% · Operational efficiency: 0.2% · Total: 1.0%+ conservative
  • Credit consumption model: Transaction volume × events/month × credits/workflow = monthly/annual cost projection
  • L3 Business Case (90-95% accurate): 60-100 pages — category-by-category strategy, 3-5 year financial model with sensitivities, phased implementation roadmap

Exit Gate

  • L3 accepted by all stakeholders → Stage 5 or Stage 6
  • Numbers questioned → Refine with client, re-present within 2 weeks

6
Post-Demo Closing Motion — Multi-Threaded Sales Execution
4-8 weeks · THE MOST CRITICAL STAGE · 6 Parallel Threads · 12 Collaterals
Enterprise deals are won or lost here. The period between "they want to buy" and "they've committed to buy." All 6 threads run IN PARALLEL, not sequentially. The sales team must manage all simultaneously.

6A — Champion Enablement (Week 1-2)

  • "Your champion is your internal sales team. If they can't sell AerChain when you're NOT in the room, the deal dies."
  • Internal business case deck — NOT your sales deck. THEIR deck, in THEIR language, with THEIR numbers from L2/L3
  • ROI summary one-pager for CFO: "$28M Year 1 savings, 8-month payback, 12.5x 3-year ROI"
  • Technical architecture summary for CIO
  • "Why Now" arguments: Cost of delay per month, competitive risk, fiscal year timing
  • Top 10 internal objection answers — Pre-scripted responses for your champion

6B — Multi-Stakeholder Threading (Week 1-6)

  • CPO / VP Sourcing: "Your team does 4-5x more strategic work" — Platform demo, case studies
  • CFO / Finance: "8-month payback, 12.5x 3-year ROI" — L3 ROI model, financial case
  • CIO / IT: "SOC2 Type II, NSE-cleared, 16-week integration" — Security brief, architecture diagram
  • CEO / MD: "Procurement as competitive advantage" — Executive brief, transformation narrative
  • Procurement Leads: "Cars24: 99% adoption. Makes your job easier." — User testimonials, training roadmap
  • Legal: "Standard MSA, GDPR-compliant DPA" — Contract templates, compliance docs

6C — IT/Security Evaluation (Week 1-8) — #1 Deal Blocker

  • Start as EARLY as possible — ideally during Stage 2/3. This is the #1 deal blocker in enterprise sales.
  • Security questionnaire responses (200-500 questions, pre-filled templates, 1-2 week turnaround)
  • Architecture review session with IT team + SOC2 Type II report + ISO27001 + GDPR + DPA
  • Penetration testing results (if required) + data residency + encryption discussion
  • Reference: "NSE cleared us after 6-7 months of the most rigorous security evaluation in Indian financial services"

6D — Reference Calls (Week 2-4)

  • Peer-to-Peer (CPO to CPO): Cars24/Anupam — "99% adoption in 3 months"
  • Practitioner-to-Practitioner: Day-to-day experience, UI, adoption stories
  • IT-to-IT: Integration, security eval, performance — for when CIO is blocking
  • CFO-to-CFO: Manjushree/Ayanish — "4.5% savings" — for when budget is the barrier
  • Protocol: Pre-brief reference → Prepare prospect → AerChain in listen mode → Follow up within 24 hours

6E — Executive Alignment (Week 3-6)

  • Deploy for deals $500K+ or when CPO alone cannot approve
  • CEO-to-CEO conversation (Harsha/Gaurav → Client CEO/MD)
  • Board-ready narrative (1-page strategic vision) + cost of inaction argument

6F — POC/Pilot Decision (Week 4-8, Conditional)

  • Technical POC: 2-4 weeks, 1 category, limited users, reduced fee
  • Pilot: 3 months, Wave 1 (tail spend), full workflow, discounted Year 1
  • POC scope must be clearly defined: success criteria, timeline, what happens after

Exit Gate

  • All 6 threads converged + internal approval secured → Stage 7
  • Stalled → Identify blocking thread, escalate, re-engage
7
Commercial Proposal + Contract
2-6 weeks · Owner: Sales Lead + Legal · 15 Collaterals

Proposal Structure (15-25 pages)

  • 1. Executive Summary (2pp) — Value proposition, key outcomes, investment
  • 2. Scope (5pp) — Inclusions (P0/P1), Exclusions (P2/P3), Client responsibilities, RACI

Objection Handling
"We already have Ariba"

That's exactly why the conversation is relevant. Most of our best customers came FROM Ariba. They weren't looking to replace it — they were looking to get value from areas Ariba couldn't reach. We typically start as a complement: handling the 60-70% of spend that Ariba struggles with — tail spend, indirect categories, services procurement. Once teams see the AI-driven insights and day-one value, the conversation about expanding naturally follows.

"Ariba has a bigger network"

Network size ≠ network value. Having 5 million suppliers in a directory doesn't help you negotiate better with YOUR specific 500 vendors. Our AI identifies savings in YOUR specific spend data — not generic benchmarks from a massive, undifferentiated network. We focus on depth of intelligence, not breadth of directory listings.

"Our IT standardized on SAP"

Aerchain's API-first architecture means we complement your SAP investment, not compete with it. We integrate natively with SAP ERP — no middleware, no custom connectors. Your IT team will appreciate that we're designed to work alongside SAP, not replace the ecosystem. Many of our customers run SAP ERP + Aerchain procurement modules together.

"Switching costs are too high"

What's the cost of NOT switching? Let's calculate your Ariba TCO vs. Aerchain TCO over 3 years — licenses, SI fees, internal team time, opportunity cost of delayed insights. Our day-one value approach means you start seeing returns immediately, not after a 12-month implementation. The switching cost argument assumes a waterfall migration — we do parallel deployment, so risk is near zero.

The V5 Transformation

From Platform to Product

V5 is the most significant architectural and philosophical shift in Aerchain's history. Every sales conversation must reflect these principles.

The V1-V4 Problem: Infinitely configurable "platform" architecture caused 50% of all bugs from configuration errors, 16-week implementation cycles, customer confusion about what they were buying, and a sales team selling "anything is possible" instead of specific value. This cascaded into long onboarding, high support costs, and customer churn from unmet expectations.
Principle 01
Product-First, Not Platform
Aerchain is a product with predefined workflows, not a configurable platform. Customers buy modules that work out of the box.
WHY THIS MATTERS
V1-V4's "platform" approach caused 50% of all bugs from configuration errors. Customers were confused about what they were buying. Sales sold "anything is possible" and delivery couldn't keep up. V5 eliminates this entirely — every module ships complete, tested, and ready to use.
Sell: Modules Not: Platform
Principle 02
Predefined Everything
Field dictionary, workflow sequences, approval matrices, report templates — all predefined. No "configure from scratch."
WHAT'S PREDEFINED
350+ field definitions · 40+ workflow templates · 15 industry-specific approval matrices · 25+ report templates · 9 intelligent channel routing rules · Standard T&C libraries by industry. The customer configures 10-15% of settings — everything else works immediately.
Principle 03
Day-One Value
Replaces the 16-week implementation cycle. Customers see value on day one through pre-configured industry templates and AI-driven setup.
IMPACT vs. COMPETITION
SAP Ariba: 6-12 months to first value. Coupa: 4-6 months. Aerchain V5: Day 1. Upload spend data → AI classifies categories → Orchestration routes first transaction → Value realized before the kickoff meeting ends. This is the single most powerful competitive differentiator in every sales conversation.
Principle 04
Microservices Architecture
Each module is an independent microservice. Buy what you need. Scale independently. No monolithic dependencies.
SALES IMPACT
Enables "land and expand" — start with 1-2 modules (e.g., Sourcing + Vendor Management), prove ROI, then add Contract Management, Spend Analytics, etc. Each module has its own pricing, its own deployment timeline, and its own success metrics. No "all or nothing" deals that scare CFOs.
Principle 05
Test-Driven Development
Every feature starts with tests. Zero tolerance for regression. Quality is non-negotiable.
WHY SALES CARES
When IT/CIO asks "How do we know this won't break?" — the answer is TDD. Every release is regression-tested before deployment. Zero downtime upgrades. This directly addresses the #1 CIO objection: stability and reliability in production environments. NSE's 6-7 month security eval validated this approach.
Principle 06
AI-First Development
AI is not a feature — it's the foundation. Every workflow has AI augmentation. Intelligence is embedded, not bolted on.
140+ AI AGENTS ACROSS 5 THEMES
Spend Classification Agent · Vendor Analysis Agent · Negotiation Strategy Agent · RFP Normalization Agent · Contract Review Agent. Key differentiator: Ariba/Coupa bolt AI onto existing workflows. Aerchain's workflows ARE AI — every transaction is intelligently routed, every response is AI-analyzed, every recommendation is data-driven.
Principle 07
Value Engineering Pods
Replaces siloed handoffs between sales → pre-sales → implementation. Cross-functional pods own the entire customer journey.
POD COMPOSITION
Each pod: Sales Lead + Solutions Architect + Business Analyst + Delivery Lead. Same team from discovery to go-live. No handoff = no information loss = no "that's not what we were promised." Customers deal with one team, one relationship, one accountability chain throughout the entire lifecycle.
Principle 08
Agentic Future of Procurement
AI agents that autonomously handle procurement tasks — PO creation, supplier communication, spend analysis, contract review. The future is autonomous.
THE ROADMAP TO AUTONOMY
Today: Human-in-the-loop AI (assists decisions, you approve). Next: Supervised autonomy (AI executes, you review). Future: Full autonomy for routine procurement (60% of transactions). Unilever is already live with AI negotiation agent. This is the "iPhone moment" for procurement — sell the vision, deliver the value.

Sales Doctrine

The 10 Commandments

Non-negotiable rules for every Aerchain sales engagement. Violate these at your own risk.

1
Thou shalt not sell the product suite as a platform
Sell specific modules that solve specific problems. Never position Aerchain as an infinitely configurable platform — that's the V1-V4 trap.
2
Thou shalt lead with value, not features
Always start with the business outcome. Features are evidence, not the headline. Lead with savings, speed, and strategic advantage.
3
Thou shalt own the timeline
Dictate next steps. Never leave scheduling to the prospect. 'I'll send you a calendar invite for Thursday at 2pm' — not 'let me know when works.'
4
Thou shalt multi-thread
Always have 3+ contacts in every deal. Never be single-threaded. Your champion can leave, get promoted, or lose interest. Map the buying committee early.
5
Thou shalt quantify everything
Every claim must have a number behind it. '60% process time reduction' — not 'significant improvement.' Data beats adjectives.
6
Thou shalt never demo without discovery
Discovery first, always. Understand their pain before showing the cure. A demo without context is a feature tour — and feature tours don't close deals.
7
Thou shalt create urgency
Every deal needs a 'Why Now' argument. FOMO, competitive pressure, upcoming budget cycles, regulatory deadlines — find the trigger.
8
Thou shalt document obsessively
Every meeting, every commitment, every risk — in writing. If it's not documented, it didn't happen. Avoma for calls, written recaps for everything.
9
Thou shalt know the competition
Cold, hard, factual competitive intelligence. Know their strengths (acknowledge them) and their weaknesses (exploit them). Never badmouth — outclass.
10
Thou shalt protect pricing integrity
Never discount without getting something in return. Multi-year commitment, case study rights, referral agreement — every discount has a trade.
